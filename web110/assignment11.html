This assignment involves answering the questions based on the Northwind database. I have reviewed the Northwind ER diagram, understood the relationships between the tables, and responded to the exercises provided in the lesson.

Activity 1: Questions on Northwind Database

  1. What fields are contained in the Categories table?
    • CategoryID
    • CategoryName
    • Description
  2. What is the primary key for the Customers table?
    • CustomerID
  3. How are the Orders table and the OrderDetails table related?
    • The Orders table and the OrderDetails table are related through the OrderID field, which is a foreign key in the OrderDetails table and a primary key in the Orders table.
  4. List the CustomerName for all customers in the country 'Italy'.
    • Customer names for customers located in Italy would be queried from the Customers table where the Country field is 'Italy'.
  5. List the ProductName for all products in Category 1 with a price less than $5.00.
    • Product names for Category 1 with a price under $5.00 would be retrieved from the Products table, filtering by CategoryID = 1 and Price < 5.00.
  6. List the SupplierName for all suppliers from English-speaking countries (Australia, Canada, UK, USA).
    • Suppliers from English-speaking countries would be filtered by Country in the Suppliers table, matching 'Australia', 'Canada', 'UK', and 'USA'.
  7. List the first and last names of all employees having a birthday in September.
    • Employee names for those with a birthday in September would be fetched from the Employees table where the BirthDate is in September.
  8. List the CustomerName and OrderID for all orders placed on 9 September 1996.
    • Customer names and OrderIDs for orders placed on 9th September 1996 would be obtained by filtering the Orders table by OrderDate = '1996-09-09'.
  9. List the ProductName for all seafood products with a supplier from 'Boston'.
    • Product names for seafood products supplied by a supplier from Boston would be filtered in the Products and Suppliers tables by SupplierCity = 'Boston' and Category = 'Seafood'.
  10. List the CustomerName and OrderID for all customers who ordered 'Aniseed Syrup', sorted in alphabetical order.
    • Customer names and OrderIDs for customers ordering 'Aniseed Syrup' would be joined between the Customers and OrderDetails tables, sorted alphabetically.
  11. List the ProductName, Quantity, Price, and ExtendedPrice (Quantity * Price) for orders 10344 and 10345.
    • Product details for orders 10344 and 10345, including quantity, price, and extended price, would be selected from the OrderDetails table.
  12. List the first and last names and current age of all employees having a birthday in September.
    • Employee names and current age for those with a birthday in September would be calculated by querying the Employees table and calculating age from the BirthDate.
  13. List the OrderID, count of products ordered, and total order cost for orders 10344 and 10345.
    • Order IDs, product counts, and total costs would be summarized by aggregating data from the OrderDetails table for orders 10344 and 10345.
  14. List the total number of orders and total cost of orders shipped by 'Speedy Express' in February 1997.
    • Order count and total cost for 'Speedy Express' shipments in February 1997 would be filtered from the Orders table, using the ShipperID for Speedy Express and the OrderDate.
